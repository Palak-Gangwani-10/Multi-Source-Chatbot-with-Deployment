Cityscape
Rashtrapati Bhavan
is the official residence of the
President of India
.
Much of New Delhi, planned by the leading 20th-century British architect
Edwin Lutyens
, was laid out to be the central administrative area of the city as a testament to Britain's imperial ambitions. New Delhi is structured around two central
promenades
called the
Rajpath
and the
Janpath
. The Rajpath, or King's Way, stretches from the
Rashtrapati Bhavan
to the
India Gate
. The
Janpath
(Hindi:
राजपथ
,
lit.
'
Path of the People
'
), formerly Queen's Way, begins at
Connaught Circus
and cuts the Rajpath at right angles. Nineteen foreign embassies are located on the nearby Shantipath (Hindi:
शांतिपथ
,
lit.
'
Path of Peace
'
), making it the largest diplomatic enclave in India.
